Accessibility

What is UI Accessibility?

It is a designers responsibility to make sure that everyone has access to what is on the website. Website accessibility lawsuits can be filed if a website is not accessible enough. Studies show that accessible websites have better search results and reach a bigger audience.

Color Contrast

Color contrast is an often overlooked web accessibility problem. People who have low vision could find it difficult to read text from a background color if it has low contrast. It is critical to consider the sufficient contrast between text and backgrounds. There are many ways to check the quality of the color contrast but for example this website https://coolors.co/contrast-checker is a good one.

Colors to differentiate Information

Using only color to differentiate things might make color blind people confused, it’s important to not use only color to make critical information understandable. People with low visual acuity or color blindness will have a hard time understanding your content. Try to use an indicator other than color such as text labels or patterns.

Alt Tags

Alt tags stands for Alternative tags. It reads out what is on a photo if an individual can’t see it. There are two ways that you can present alternative text. Within the attribute of the image element or within context or surroundings of the image itself.

Focus States

Focus indicators help people know which element has the keyboard focus and help them understand where they are when navigating your site. These are used by people who are blind and require screen readers, individuals with limited mobility, individuals who have suffered injuries like carpal tunnel, and power users who prefer this type of navigation.
